Da Stu's Dancing Style Homepage

Welcome to my ballroom dancing homepage. Here, I document some of the dances I've been learning so far in my ballroom dancing career.

What is the Difference Between Standard and Latin?

I've categorised each dance into the two broad categories of "Standard" ballroom dances and "Latin". The standard ballrooms tend to involve a stiffer and closer "frame" (how the two dancers hold eachother), and also usually involve the couple moving around the edges of the ballroom.

Latin dances, however, usually have a looser frame and dancers tend to stay around the middle of the dance floor. Latin dances tend to have more hip movement and are danced to a wider variety of music.

What Does My Rating System Mean?

For each dance, I've provided a rating on the classic five-star scale. This rating doesn't attempt to provide an objective level of quality for each dance, but simply reflects my personal enjoyment of the dance. A five-star dance is one I find myself coming back to time and time again, whilst a one-star dance is one I rarely come back to, prefering others. It's not that I believe the one-star dance is bad but just that, for whatever reason, I don't personally enjoy it.
